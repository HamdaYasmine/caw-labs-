Brief History

Palestine has a rich and ancient history dating back thousands of years, with archaeological evidence showing human presence since prehistoric times. The land has been home to various civilizations including Canaanites, Israelites, Romans, Byzantines, and Arabs.

In the modern era, Palestine was part of the Ottoman Empire until World War I, after which it came under British administration. The 1948 Arab-Israeli war led to the establishment of Israel and the displacement of hundreds of thousands of Palestinians, an event known as the Nakba (catastrophe).

Today, Palestine is recognized as a sovereign state by many countries and international organizations. The Palestinian people continue to strive for self-determination and independence, with ongoing efforts to establish a viable Palestinian state alongside Israel.
